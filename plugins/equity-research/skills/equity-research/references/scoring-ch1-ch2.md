# Scoring Criteria — Chapters 1 & 2: Fundamental Quality + Valuation

> Score [1–5]. Score reflects the **weaker** of sub-dimensions. 🚩 = RED FLAG: score of 1 triggers automatic verdict downgrade.

---

## Chapter 1: Fundamental Quality (Weight: 35%)

### 1.1 — Revenue Growth & Trajectory [1–5] 🚩 RED FLAG

**What it measures:** Top-line growth rate AND whether growth is accelerating, stable, or decelerating.

| Score | Criteria |
|:---:|---|
| 5 | Growth significantly above sector "Strong" threshold AND accelerating. Multi-year beat-and-raise. |
| 4 | Growth above sector "Strong" threshold, stable trajectory. Consistent beats. |
| 3 | Growth within sector "Adequate" corridor. Stable, roughly in line with estimates. |
| 2 | Growth below "Adequate" floor OR clear deceleration (3+ consecutive quarters declining). Missing estimates. |
| 1 | Revenue declining/stagnant. Structural demand problem evident. |

**Red flag triggers:** Revenue decline 2+ consecutive quarters without cyclical explanation. Organic growth negative while M&A masks decline.

**Key calculation:** Revenue CAGR (1yr, 3yr), sequential QoQ growth, organic vs. acquisition-driven.

---

### 1.2 — Margin Profile & Trend [1–5] 🚩 RED FLAG

**What it measures:** Current margin levels relative to sector peers AND direction over past 8–12 quarters.

| Score | Criteria |
|:---:|---|
| 5 | Margins at/above sector "Strong" on all levels. Expanding. Operating leverage improving. |
| 4 | Margins above average. Stable or modestly expanding. |
| 3 | Margins within "Adequate" corridor. Stable. No clear trend. |
| 2 | Margins below average OR compressing on 2+ levels. |
| 1 | Margins significantly below peers AND compressing. Gross margin erosion = pricing power loss. |

**Red flag triggers:** Gross margin declining 200+ bps over 4Q without investment explanation. Adjusted EBITDA wildly divergent from GAAP operating income.

**Key calculation:** Gross/operating/EBITDA/net margin — current and 3yr trend. Incremental margin = Δ operating income / Δ revenue.

---

### 1.3 — Return on Invested Capital (ROIC) [1–5]

**What it measures:** Returns above cost of capital. ROIC > WACC = value creation.

| Score | Criteria |
|:---:|---|
| 5 | ROIC consistently > 2x WACC. Top-quartile. Stable/improving 3+ years. |
| 4 | ROIC > 1.5x WACC. Above sector median. Stable. |
| 3 | ROIC ≈ WACC (0.8–1.2x). Meeting cost of capital. |
| 2 | ROIC below WACC. May be cyclical/temporary. |
| 1 | ROIC persistently below WACC 3+ years. No credible improvement path. |

**Key calculation:** ROIC = NOPAT / Invested Capital. NOPAT = Operating Income × (1 − tax rate). Compare to WACC (~7–11% for most large-caps).

---

### 1.4 — Cash Flow Quality & Conversion [1–5]

**What it measures:** FCF conversion (FCF/Net Income) — whether earnings translate to cash.

| Score | Criteria |
|:---:|---|
| 5 | FCF conversion > 100% consistently. FCF growing faster than earnings. Low capex intensity. |
| 4 | FCF conversion 80–100%. FCF growth in line with earnings. |
| 3 | FCF conversion 60–80%. Some drag from working capital or capex. |
| 2 | FCF conversion < 60% OR negative FCF despite profitability. |
| 1 | Persistently negative FCF in mature business. Cash consumption accelerating. |

**Key calculation:** FCF = Operating CF − Capex. FCF margin = FCF/Revenue. FCF yield = FCF/Market Cap. Accrual ratio = (NI − Operating CF)/Total Assets.

---

### 1.5 — Balance Sheet Strength [1–5]

**What it measures:** Financial resilience — leverage, liquidity, debt maturity.

| Score | Criteria |
|:---:|---|
| 5 | Net cash or < 0.5x EBITDA. Current ratio > 2x. No maturity wall. Investment grade. |
| 4 | Net debt/EBITDA < 1.5x. Comfortable liquidity. BBB+ or better. |
| 3 | Leverage within sector "Adequate" corridor. Adequate liquidity. |
| 2 | Leverage approaching "Weak" threshold. Tightening liquidity. Refinancing risk. |
| 1 | Overleveraged. Liquidity strained. Maturity wall without refinancing path. Covenant risk. |

**Sector notes:** Banks: use CET1. REITs: higher leverage normal. Utilities: regulated = higher leverage tolerated.

---

### 1.6 — Earnings Quality & Predictability [1–5]

**What it measures:** Reliability, transparency, sustainability of reported earnings.

| Score | Criteria |
|:---:|---|
| 5 | Highly predictable (recurring/subscription). Minimal adjustments. Consistent beats. Low volatility. |
| 4 | Mostly predictable. Reasonable adjustments. Consistent with cash flow. |
| 3 | Adequate. Some lumpiness. Adjustments present but reasonable. |
| 2 | Significant GAAP/non-GAAP gap. Recurring restructuring. Aggressive recognition. Frequent misses. |
| 1 | Opaque. Persistent large gaps. Restatements. Auditor concerns. Unpredictable. |

**Warning signals:** SBC > 15% of revenue, adjusted EBITDA > 30% above GAAP operating income, DSO growing faster than revenue.

## Chapter 2: Valuation (Weight: 20%)

### 2.1 — Absolute Valuation [1–5]

**What it measures:** Whether multiples (P/E, EV/EBITDA, P/FCF) are reasonable for the growth rate and quality.

| Score | Criteria |
|:---:|---|
| 5 | All primary multiples below sector average despite above-average growth/quality. Clear undervaluation. |
| 4 | Multiples at/slightly below sector average with above-average fundamentals. |
| 3 | Multiples at sector average with average fundamentals. PEG 1.0–1.5x. |
| 2 | Multiples above sector average without superior fundamentals. PEG > 2.0. |
| 1 | Extreme premium > 2x sector average. Priced for perfection. |

---

### 2.2 — Relative Valuation vs. Peers [1–5]

**What it measures:** Premium/discount to 3–5 closest comparables, adjusted for growth/profitability.

| Score | Criteria |
|:---:|---|
| 5 | Discount > 15% to peers despite comparable/superior fundamentals. |
| 4 | Modest discount (5–15%) with competitive fundamentals. |
| 3 | In line with peers (±5%). No mispricing. |
| 2 | Modest premium (5–20%) without clearly superior fundamentals. |
| 1 | Premium > 20%. Fundamentals don't justify the spread. |

---

### 2.3 — Historical Valuation Range [1–5]

**What it measures:** Current valuation vs. company's own 5-year trading range.

| Score | Criteria |
|:---:|---|
| 5 | Below 5yr avg by > 1 std dev AND fundamentals NOT deteriorated. |
| 4 | Below 5yr avg. Fundamentals stable/improving. Room for normalization. |
| 3 | Within ±0.5 std dev of 5yr avg. Historical norm. |
| 2 | Above 5yr avg by > 0.5 std dev without fundamental improvement. |
| 1 | At/above 5yr highs on multiple basis. Mean-reversion risk elevated. |

---

### 2.4 — DCF / Intrinsic Value Gap [1–5]

**What it measures:** Gap between market price and DCF intrinsic value.

| Score | Criteria |
|:---:|---|
| 5 | DCF fair value > 30% above current price (conservative assumptions). Large margin of safety. |
| 4 | DCF fair value 15–30% above current price. Meaningful upside. |
| 3 | DCF fair value within ±15%. Fairly valued. |
| 2 | DCF fair value 15–30% below current price. Market pricing optimism. |
| 1 | DCF fair value > 30% below current price. Severe overvaluation. |

**Methodology:** Use conservative growth (below guidance), terminal growth 2–4%, appropriate WACC. See valuation-methods.md.
