# Analyst Checklist — Chapters 3 & 4: Competitive Position + Growth Catalysts

> What to pull from MCP, what to search, how to assess.

---

## Chapter 3: Competitive Position & Moat

### 3.1–3.4 — All Competitive Criteria

**MCP calls:**
- `get_stock_info` → Sector, Industry (competitive context)
- `get_yahoo_finance_news` → Recent competitive developments
- `get_recommendations` → Analyst consensus and recent changes

**Web search (critical for this chapter):**
- "[Company] market share [industry]" — position data
- "[Company] vs [Competitor 1] vs [Competitor 2]" — competitive comparison
- "[Company] competitive advantages" or "[Company] moat"
- "[Industry] competitive landscape [year]" — industry structure
- "[Company] customer switching costs" — retention/stickiness evidence
- "[Company] pricing power" or "[Company] price increases"

**Key questions:**
- **Moat:** Which of the 5 moat types? Be specific — what does the brand allow? Price premium? Higher retention? Lower CAC?
- **Durability:** Widening (network effects growing, switching costs increasing) or narrowing (tech commoditizing, new entrants)?
- **Share:** Gaining or losing? Compare revenue growth to total market growth.
- **Pricing power:** Above-inflation increases without churn? Historical gross margin during inflation?
- **Threats:** Most dangerous competitor? Disruption likely within 5 years?

## Chapter 4: Growth Catalysts & Optionality

### 4.1–4.3 — All Catalyst Criteria

**MCP calls:**
- `get_yahoo_finance_news` → Recent catalysts, announcements
- `get_recommendations(upgrades_downgrades)` → Recent rating changes signal catalyst views
- `get_stock_info` → Forward estimates imply market's growth expectation

**Web search:**
- "[Company] product pipeline [year]" — upcoming launches
- "[Company] earnings call highlights" — management commentary on drivers
- "[Company] TAM addressable market" — market sizing
- "[Company] geographic expansion" — new market entry
- "[Company] M&A strategy" — acquisition-driven growth
- "[Industry] secular trends" — long-term tailwinds/headwinds

**Key questions:**
- **Near-term (0–12mo):** Specific events? Earnings beats, launches, regulatory decisions, contracts. Be concrete.
- **Medium-term (1–3yr):** Growth engine? New products, geo expansion, pricing, share gains? Execution evidence?
- **Long-term:** TAM expanding/contracting? Platform adjacency potential? Secular tailwinds/headwinds?
- **Consensus:** What is the market already pricing in? Will actual growth EXCEED expectations?
